## 💬 Discovery Call Script (15 minutes)

### Opening (2 minutes)

**You:** "Thanks for making time! Before I show you GitLog, can you tell me a bit about how you currently handle release communication?"

**Listen for:**

- Manual changelog writing
- Inconsistent updates
- User complaints about not knowing what's new
- Time spent writing release notes

**You:** "Got it. And how often would you say you ship new features?"

**You:** "On a scale of 1-10, how painful is release communication for you right now?"

---

### Problem Agitation (3 minutes)

**You:** "So if I'm hearing you correctly:

- You're shipping [X features/week]
- But users don't know what's changed
- And writing changelogs manually takes [Y hours/week]

Is that fair?"

**You:** "What happens when you don't communicate releases? Do users complain? Do you miss out on showing progress to stakeholders?"

**You:** "And if you had to guess, how much time have you spent writing changelogs in the last month?"

---

### Solution Presentation (5 minutes)

**You:** "Let me show you how GitLog solves this..."

**[Share screen, show GitLog dashboard]**

**You:** "Here's what the workflow looks like:

1. **Connect GitHub** (30 seconds)
   - One OAuth click
   - Select your repo

2. **Merge PRs as usual** (no change to your workflow)
   - GitLog watches for merges
   - Draft appears in dashboard <30s

3. **Optional: AI rewrite** (1 click)
   - Technical PR → user-friendly language
   - Choose tone: Professional, Casual, Exciting

4. **Publish** (1 click)
   - Live on public changelog
   - Auto-generates social posts
   - Sends email digest to subscribers

Total time per release: 2 minutes max. Usually less."

---

### Handle Objections (3 minutes)

**Objection:** "We already use GitHub Releases"

**Response:** "GitHub Releases is great for developers, but it's technical and stays on GitHub. GitLog creates a user-facing changelog that your customers actually see. Plus, it auto-generates social posts and emails—GitHub doesn't do that."

---

**Objection:** "We're small, don't need a changelog yet"

**Response:** "That's actually the perfect time to start! When you're small, every user notices your attention to detail. A public changelog shows you're shipping consistently and care about communication. It's way harder to add later once you have users expecting updates."

---

**Objection:** "We tried changelogs before, didn't stick"

**Response:** "That's exactly why we built GitLog! Most founders quit because manual writing is tedious. GitLog removes the friction—your changelog updates automatically from PRs you already merged. No extra writing, no context-switching to another tool."

---

**Objection:** "$19/month is steep for a changelog"

**Response:** "Fair question. Let's break it down:

- Canny: $49/month (no auto-sync)
- Featurebase: $29/month (no auto-sync)
- Frill: $49/month (no auto-sync)

We're 60% cheaper, and we're the only ones with GitHub auto-sync + AI rewrites + social posts.

Plus, if GitLog saves you 2 hours/month of manual writing, that's $9.50/hour. Most founders value their time way higher than that."

---

### Close (2 minutes)

**You:** "Based on what you've seen, does GitLog make sense for [Company]?"

**If yes:** "Awesome! I can set you up with a free account right now. 50 entries/month, no credit card. Want to do it together?"

**If hesitant:** "No pressure at all. How about this—I'll give you a 14-day Pro trial, no strings. Use all the features, see if it sticks. If it's not a fit, just cancel. Fair?"

**If no:** "Totally understand. Can I ask—what's the main thing holding you back? [Listen] Got it. Well, if anything changes, you know where to find us. Thanks for your time!"
